# Policy Landing Page

Welcome to our central landing page for Hypha's policies related to health & safety, respect in the workplace, accessibility, and more. These policies are living documents rooted in our values and responsibilities, designed to guide how we work, engage, and take care of one another.

**Purpose of Our Policies**  
Our policies are not just about compliance. They are tools for action, clarity, and care. They are designed to help us uphold **human dignity, mutual respect, and psychological and physical safety** in every interaction, process, and space we operate in.

While each policy outlines specific responsibilities and procedures, this page offers a shared foundation: one that affirms our collective commitment to equity, accountability, and the legislative frameworks that guide our work in Canada.

By placing our values and commitments here, we keep the policies themselves focused on clear, actionable guidance,  making it easier for everyone to understand and uphold them.

**Who These Policies Apply To**  
When we refer to **"everyone at Hypha",** we mean:

* All employees, members,  contractors, clients, collaborators, and any individuals or communities we work with, serve, or engage with through our programs, services, or operations.

This definition ensures our policies support **safe, respectful, and accessible** environments for all who are part of our ecosystem.

**Our Commitments**  
We are committed to:

* **Human dignity, respect, and inclusion** in all our spaces \- virtual and physical.  
* **Safety, accessibility, and well-being** as non-negotiable foundations of how we operate.  
* **Responsiveness and accountability**, especially when harm occurs.  
* **Upholding Canadian legislation**, particularly in:  
  * Ontario: including the Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA), the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA), and the Ontario Human Rights Code.  
  * Québec: including the Act respecting occupational health and safety (AOHS), Bill 42: An Act to prevent and fight psychological harassment and sexual violence in the workplace and the Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms.  
  * *Federal and national frameworks: as applicable and as we grow.*

These commitments guide not only our internal practices but also how we show up with and for our broader community.
